## Intuition

Most people consuming data are not trying to “mine the lake” for sport—they need **the right dataset**, **for the right purpose**, **with justified trust**, **on time**. Without shared rules and shared context, every request becomes a **project**: ask around, open tickets, trace spreadsheets, guess which table is canonical, and hope nobody renamed a column last week.

**Data governance** is the deliberate practice of making data **legible, accountable, and usable** across teams—so consumers spend less time **hunting** and more time **deciding**.

<KeyIdea>
  Strong data governance **transforms the consumer experience** from a **scavenger hunt**—opaque, ad hoc, and relationship-dependent—into a **structured marketplace**: **discoverable** assets, clear **terms of use**, visible **quality and lineage**, and **repeatable access** within policy.
</KeyIdea>

## The scavenger hunt (what it feels like)

- **Discovery by rumor:** “I think finance uses `revenue_v3_final_FINAL`—or was it `rev_hist_2`?”
- **Trust by charisma:** credibility comes from **who** told you, not **what** is documented.
- **Integration tax:** every new question repeats the same glue work—access requests, format surprises, unknown freshness.
- **Fragile wins:** the one analyst who “knows where everything is” becomes a **single point of failure**.

This pattern **does not scale** with more data, more tools, or more regulation—it scales **confusion**.

## The structured marketplace (what good looks like)

A marketplace is not “more dashboards.” It is a **system** where consumers can:

- **Browse** a catalog of **data products** or datasets with **purpose**, **owner**, and **SLAs**.
- **Read the label:** definitions, grain, refresh cadence, known gaps, and **classification** (PII, confidential, etc.).
- **See lineage:** where numbers come from and what downstream depends on them.
- **Request access** through **consistent workflows**—policy enforced **by design**, not by hallway negotiation.

The emotional shift is the same as moving from **craigslist free-for-all** to **a store with prices, returns, and ingredients listed**—less adrenaline, more **reliability**.

## What governance brings to the table (in plain terms)

- **Business glossary & metadata** so the same words mean the same things.
- **Data catalog + ownership** so questions route to **accountable** owners.
- **Lineage & impact analysis** so changes are less scary.
- **Quality rules & monitoring** so “trust” is **observed**, not assumed.
- **Access and classification policy** so compliance is **embedded**, not bolted on after the fact.

None of these are “one tool”—they are **capabilities** that mature together.

<ConceptSteps>
  <ConceptStep title="Make inventory visible">
    You cannot shop in a market you cannot see. A catalog (even incremental) is the
    first step out of scavenger mode.
  </ConceptStep>
  <ConceptStep title="Attach meaning and policy">
    Definitions, sensitivity, ownership, and SLAs turn “files” into **products**
    people can evaluate.
  </ConceptStep>
  <ConceptStep title="Measure trust">
    Freshness checks, quality checks, and lineage make reliability **legible**—not
    a private opinion.
  </ConceptStep>
</ConceptSteps>

## What to remember

- **Scavenger hunt** = opaque discovery + **heroic** knowledge work.
- **Marketplace** = discoverable assets + **documented** meaning + **policy** + **repeatable** access.
- Governance is how you **scale** data use without scaling **chaos**.
